## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Breach Response and Incident Management Plan ("Plan") establishes the procedures that Metzler Foundations will follow to respond to a breach of security involving Protected Health Information (PHI) or other sensitive data. This Plan ensures compliance with HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR ยง 164.400-414) and other applicable laws and regulations.

### 1.2 Scope

This Plan applies to:

- All Metzler Foundations employees, contractors, and agents
- All systems, applications, and data repositories containing PHI
- All incidents involving unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of PHI
- Incidents occurring on premises, remotely, or involving third parties

---

## 2. Definitions

### 2.1 Breach

"Breach" means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of protected health information (PHI) in a manner not permitted under the HIPAA Privacy Rule which compromises the security or privacy of such information.

### 2.2 Protected Health Information (PHI)

Any information about health status, provision of health care, or payment for health care that can be linked to an individual, including demographic information.

### 2.3 Security Incident

Any attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations.

### 2.4 Privacy Incident

Any attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of PHI.

## 5. Breach Detection and Reporting

### 5.1 Detection Methods

Breaches may be detected through:

- Automated security monitoring systems
- User reports of suspicious activity
- Audit log reviews
- Third-party notifications
- Law enforcement notifications

### 5.2 Reporting Requirements

Any individual who discovers or suspects a breach must immediately report it to:

- Privacy Officer: privacy@metzlerfoundations.org or (555) 123-4567
- Compliance Officer: compliance@metzlerfoundations.org
- IT Security Officer: security@metzlerfoundations.org

**No investigation should begin without involving the Privacy Officer.**

### 5.3 Initial Assessment Timeline

- **Immediate (within 1 hour):** Initial report to Privacy Officer
- **Within 24 hours:** Preliminary breach determination
- **Within 7 days:** Final breach determination and notification plan

## 6. Breach Investigation Process

### 6.1 Step 1: Secure the Scene

- Isolate affected systems
- Preserve evidence (do not alter systems)
- Document initial findings
- Notify IT Security Officer immediately

### 6.2 Step 2: Initial Assessment (Within 24 hours)

The Breach Response Team will:

- Review available information about the incident
- Determine if PHI was involved
- Assess the scope and potential impact
- Make preliminary determination if breach occurred

### 6.3 Step 3: Investigation (Within 7 days)

- Conduct thorough forensic analysis
- Interview relevant personnel
- Review audit logs and access records
- Determine root cause and contributing factors
- Quantify affected individuals and data elements

### 6.4 Step 4: Final Determination

- Document findings in Breach Investigation Report
- Determine if notification is required
- Develop remediation plan

---

## 7. Containment and Mitigation

### 7.1 Immediate Containment

- Disconnect affected systems from network
- Change all compromised credentials
- Implement additional security controls
- Notify affected business associates

### 7.2 Evidence Preservation

- Secure all relevant logs and data
- Document system state at time of breach
- Preserve digital evidence for forensic analysis
- Maintain chain of custody

### 7.3 System Recovery

- Clean and restore affected systems
- Verify system integrity
- Implement additional security measures
- Test restored systems before production use

---

## 8. Notification Requirements

### 8.1 Individual Notifications

**When:** Within 60 days of breach discovery
**To:** All individuals whose PHI was compromised
**Method:** Written notification via:

- First-class mail
- Email (if individual has agreed to electronic communications)
- Telephone (for urgent situations)

**Content Requirements:**

- Description of breach
- Types of PHI involved
- Steps individuals should take
- Contact information for questions
- Steps Metzler Foundations is taking

### 8.2 HHS Notification

**When:** Within 60 days of breach discovery
**To:** U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)
**Method:** Online portal at https://ocrportal.hhs.gov/

**Content Requirements:**

- Breach description and timeline
- Number of affected individuals
- Types of PHI involved
- Actions taken in response

### 8.3 Media Notification

**When:** Within 60 days if 500+ individuals affected
**Method:** Press release to major media outlets

### 8.4 Business Associate Notifications

**When:** Immediately upon discovery
**To:** All business associates involved in breach
**Purpose:** Enable their own breach response procedures

## 9. Documentation and Reporting

### 9.1 Breach Log

All breaches must be documented in the HIPAA Breach Log including:

- Date and time of discovery
- Description of breach
- Individuals affected
- Actions taken
- Resolution status

### 9.2 Breach Investigation Report

Must include:

- Executive summary
- Timeline of events
- Root cause analysis
- Impact assessment
- Remediation actions
- Prevention recommendations

### 9.3 Annual Breach Report

Submitted to HHS by April 1 each year including:

- Number of breaches
- Types of breaches
- Response actions taken
